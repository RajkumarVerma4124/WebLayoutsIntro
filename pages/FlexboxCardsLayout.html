Coolest Concept Cars Articles

BMW Vision M Next

The Vision M Next, revealed in summer 2019, is the M1 reborn for the 21st century. A mid-engine profile and swing-up doors allows it screams presence, while a 600-horsepower hybrid powertrain means it has the punch to back up those looks. Let's hope BMW has plans to put it into production.

Karma SC2

The only car Karma currently builds is its Revero sedan, which is basically a rehashed Fisker Karma. But in late 2019, it revealed this, the SC2 concept, an all-electric 1100-horsepower supercar which is supposed to represent the future of the brand. We have our reservations, but those massive scissor doors are pretty cool.

Tesla Cybertruck

No matter your opinion on Tesla or Elon Musk, you have to admit: the Cybertruck is one wild proposition. Just look at it! It's a future-mobile with a wild cyborg-like design and angles sharp enough to cut through a phonebook. We can't wait to see the production version.

Rolls-Royce 103EX

The 103EX was supposed to represent Rolls-Royce's idea for the future in 2016. Wildly different than any Rolls production car, it doesn't have a steering wheel, or even a driver's seat. The only place to sit is a massive couch; a place to relax while you're taken to your destination in ultimate comfort.

Porsche 918 Spyder

Porsche's halo car stands as a striking monument to a moment in time when hundred year-old fuel burning technology merged with electric power to create a wave of flagship vehicles that changed all the rules.

Mercedes-Maybach Vision 6 Cabriolet

If you thought the Vision 6 coupe couldn't get any better, you were probably shocked to see the Cabriolet version. It looks like a yacht on wheels, with a stunning interior and a massive rear overhang.

Opel GT

The Opel GT concept from Geneva 2016 didn't lead to a production car, but it was still incredibly neat. It had everything we want from a modern performance coupe: Great looks, tight proportions, and a low curb weight.

Mazda RX-Vision

The RX-Vision is Mazda's representation of what a future rotary-powered sports car might look like, and it's stunning. Sadly, it's still just a concept for now.

Jeep J6

Introduced at Easter Jeep Safari last year, the J6 concept is basically the two-door, long-bed Gladiator of our fantasies. The proportions are perfect, and we can't help but prefer it over the normal Gladiator.

Renault Trezor

The Trezor was released in 2016 in Paris, sporting a version of Renault's Formula E drivetrain and autonomous driving tech. But it was the stunning design that had everyone really excited. It's too bad a production version never came to fruition.

GFG Style Kangaroo

Spawned from the minds of Giorgetto Giugiario and his son, the GFG Style Kangaroo combines supercar proportions with an unconventional off-road ride height. It also has gullwing windows, which is undeniably awesome.

Volkswagen ID. Buggy

The ID. Buggy is the latest in a string of ID-badged concepts from VW, aimed at showing off the brand's new modular electric platform. We think it's the coolest of the bunch.
